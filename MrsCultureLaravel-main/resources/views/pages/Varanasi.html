Welcome to Varanasi!

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is one of the oldest inhabited cities in the world and a sacred pilgrimage site for Hindus. Located on the banks of the holy River Ganges in Uttar Pradesh, India, Varanasi is considered the spiritual capital of India.

Explore the narrow winding streets, visit ancient temples, witness the Ganga Aarti ceremony on the ghats, and experience the spirituality that permeates every corner of the city. Varanasi is a melting pot of culture, religion, and traditions, offering a unique and immersive experience to visitors.

Popular Tourist Places in Varanasi

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the holiest Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is a major pilgrimage site and a must-visit place for devotees seeking spiritual blessings.

Location
Dashashwamedh Ghat

Dashashwamedh Ghat is one of the oldest and most important ghats in Varanasi. It is known for its grand Ganga Aarti ceremony, where priests perform rituals and offer prayers to the river.

Location
Sarnath

Sarnath is an important Buddhist pilgrimage site located near Varanasi. It is where Gautam Buddha gave his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. Explore the ancient stupas, monasteries, and the famous Dhamek Stupa.
